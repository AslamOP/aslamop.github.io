Unit 1: Quantum Mechanics - 2 Mark Questions

1 What is photoelectric effect?

The photoelectric effect is the emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of suitable frequency falls on it. Key points:

  • Electrons are emitted instantly when light hits the surface
  • Emission occurs only above a threshold frequency
  • Kinetic energy of electrons depends on frequency, not intensity
Reference: Photoelectric Effect Presentation
2 State de Broglie hypothesis.

De Broglie hypothesis states that all moving particles have an associated wave nature. The wavelength λ of the matter wave is related to the momentum p of the particle by the relation:

λ = h/p = h/mv

where h is Planck's constant, m is mass, and v is velocity of the particle.

Reference: de Broglie Wave Presentation
3 State Heisenberg's uncertainty principle.

Heisenberg's uncertainty principle states that it is impossible to simultaneously measure both the position and momentum of a particle with absolute precision. The product of uncertainties in position (Δx) and momentum (Δp) is:

Δx·Δp ≥ h/4π

where h is Planck's constant.

Reference: Uncertainty Principle Presentation
4 What is wave function? What is its physical significance?

A wave function (ψ) is a mathematical function that describes the quantum state of an isolated quantum system. Its physical significance:

  • |ψ|² gives the probability density of finding the particle at a given point
  • Must be single-valued, continuous, and finite
  • Must satisfy Schrödinger wave equation
Reference: Schrödinger Equation Presentation
