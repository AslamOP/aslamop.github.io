Unit 1: Quantum Mechanics - 10 Mark Questions

1. Explain Einstein's photoelectric equation. Describe the experimental verification of photoelectric effect and its observations.

Answer Framework:

  • Introduction to photoelectric effect (1 mark)
  • Einstein's photoelectric equation and its components (2 marks)
  • Experimental setup description (2 marks)
  • Key observations and their significance (3 marks)
  • Applications and conclusion (2 marks)

Detailed Explanation:

1. Introduction

The photoelectric effect is the emission of electrons from a metal surface when electromagnetic radiation of suitable frequency falls on it. This phenomenon demonstrates the quantum nature of light.

2. Einstein's Photoelectric Equation

hν = φ + KEmax where: h = Planck's constant ν = Frequency of incident radiation φ = Work function of the metal KEmax = Maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons

3. Experimental Setup

The experimental setup consists of an evacuated glass tube containing two electrodes: a cathode (metal plate) and an anode. When light falls on the cathode, photoelectrons are emitted and collected by the anode.

4. Key Observations

  • Instantaneous emission of electrons
  • Existence of threshold frequency
  • Linear relationship between frequency and kinetic energy
  • Independence of kinetic energy from intensity

5. Applications

The photoelectric effect has numerous applications including:

  • Photoelectric cells
  • Solar panels
  • Light meters in cameras
  • Automatic door openers

2. Derive time-independent Schrödinger wave equation and explain its significance in quantum mechanics.

Answer Framework:

  • Need for wave equation in quantum mechanics (1 mark)
  • Mathematical derivation (4 marks)
  • Physical significance of wave function (2 marks)
  • Boundary conditions and constraints (2 marks)
  • Applications (1 mark)

Detailed Explanation:

1. Introduction

The Schrödinger equation is a fundamental equation in quantum mechanics that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes with time.

2. Derivation

Starting from de Broglie relation: λ = h/p Through wave equation: d²ψ/dx² + k²ψ = 0 Leading to: -ħ²/2m · d²ψ/dx² + V(x)ψ = Eψ

3. Physical Significance

  • Wave function (ψ) represents probability amplitude
  • |ψ|² gives probability density
  • Must be normalized: ∫|ψ|²dx = 1

4. Applications

Applications include:

  • Particle in a box
  • Quantum tunneling
  • Hydrogen atom model
