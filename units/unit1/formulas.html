Unit 1: Quantum Mechanics - Important Formulas

Photoelectric Effect

Einstein's Photoelectric Equation
hν = φ + KEmax
Relates the energy of incident photons to the work function and maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons
  • h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s)
  • ν = Frequency of incident light
  • φ = Work function of the metal
  • KEmax = Maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons
Practice Problems
Threshold Wavelength
λ₀ = hc/φ
Maximum wavelength of light that can cause photoelectric emission
  • λ₀ = Threshold wavelength
  • c = Speed of light (3 × 10⁸ m/s)

de Broglie Wave Theory

de Broglie Wavelength
λ = h/p = h/mv
Wavelength associated with a particle of momentum p
  • λ = de Broglie wavelength
  • p = Momentum of the particle
  • m = Mass of the particle
  • v = Velocity of the particle

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Position-Momentum Uncertainty
Δx·Δp ≥ ħ/2
Fundamental limit on simultaneous measurement of position and momentum
  • Δx = Uncertainty in position
  • Δp = Uncertainty in momentum
  • ħ = h/2π (reduced Planck's constant)
Energy-Time Uncertainty
ΔE·Δt ≥ ħ/2
Fundamental limit on simultaneous measurement of energy and time
  • ΔE = Uncertainty in energy
  • Δt = Uncertainty in time

Schrödinger Wave Equation

Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation
-ħ²/2m · d²ψ/dx² + V(x)ψ = Eψ
Describes quantum states and their corresponding energies
  • ψ = Wave function
  • V(x) = Potential energy function
  • E = Total energy
