Unit 4: Nanomaterials

10 Mark Questions

1. Explain the working principle of Atomic Force Microscope (AFM). Describe its construction and different modes of operation.

Answer:

Working Principle:

AFM works by measuring the force between a sharp tip and the sample surface. The tip is mounted on a cantilever, and as it scans the surface, the force causes the cantilever to deflect, which is measured using a laser beam.

Construction:

  1. Scanning System
    • Piezoelectric scanner
    • Sharp tip on cantilever
    • Sample stage
  2. Detection System
    • Laser diode
    • Photodetector
    • Feedback electronics

Modes of Operation:

  1. Contact Mode
    • Tip in constant contact with surface
    • Measures repulsive forces
    • High resolution but can damage soft samples
  2. Tapping Mode
    • Tip oscillates near surface
    • Measures amplitude changes
    • Better for soft samples
  3. Non-contact Mode
    • Tip oscillates above surface
    • Measures attractive forces
    • Lowest resolution but no sample damage

2. Describe the synthesis methods of quantum dots and explain their unique optical properties.

Answer:

Synthesis Methods:

  1. Colloidal Synthesis
    • Solution-based method
    • Precise control of size
    • High quality and monodispersity
  2. Molecular Beam Epitaxy
    • Layer-by-layer growth
    • High purity
    • Controlled composition
  3. Chemical Vapor Deposition
    • Gas-phase synthesis
    • Scalable production
    • Good for industrial applications

Optical Properties:

  1. Size-dependent Emission
    • Band gap varies with size
    • Emission wavelength tunable
    • Narrow emission spectra
  2. High Quantum Yield
    • Efficient light emission
    • Bright fluorescence
    • Stable against photobleaching
  3. Applications
    • LEDs and displays
    • Solar cells
    • Biological imaging
    • Optical sensors

3. Explain the properties and applications of graphene. Discuss its synthesis methods.

Answer:

Properties:

  1. Physical Properties
    • Single layer of carbon atoms
    • High mechanical strength
    • Excellent thermal conductivity
  2. Electronic Properties
    • High electron mobility
    • Zero band gap semiconductor
    • Ballistic transport
  3. Optical Properties
    • Transparent to visible light
    • High optical absorption
    • Unique Raman spectrum

Synthesis Methods:

  1. Mechanical Exfoliation
    • Peeling from graphite
    • High quality
    • Limited scalability
  2. Chemical Vapor Deposition
    • Growth on metal substrates
    • Large area production
    • Good quality
  3. Chemical Reduction
    • Reduction of graphene oxide
    • Mass production
    • Lower quality

Applications:

  • Transparent electrodes
  • High-speed electronics
  • Energy storage devices
  • Composites and coatings
  • Sensors and biosensors
