Unit 2: Laser & Fiber Optics - Formula Sheet

Essential formulas for Lasers and Fiber Optics, including Einstein coefficients, population inversion, numerical aperture, acceptance angle, and more. Use this as a quick reference for your studies and exams.

Laser Physics

B12 ρ(ν) = B21 ρ(ν) + A21
This equation relates the probabilities of absorption, stimulated emission, and spontaneous emission in a two-level system.
N2 > N1
Population inversion condition, essential for achieving net stimulated emission in lasers.
E = hν
Energy of a laser photon, where h is Planck's constant and ν is the frequency of light.

Fiber Optics

NA = sqrt(n12 - n22)
Numerical aperture, indicating the light-gathering ability of the fiber.
θmax = sin-1(NA)
Acceptance angle, the maximum angle for light to enter the fiber and be guided.
V = (2πa/λ) NA
V-number, determining the number of modes in the fiber, where a is the core radius and λ is the wavelength.
