Unit 2: Lasers & Fiber Optics

2 Mark Questions

1. What is population inversion in a laser?

Answer: Population inversion is the condition in which the number of atoms in a higher energy state exceeds those in a lower energy state, which is essential for laser action.

2. Differentiate between spontaneous and stimulated emission.

Answer: Spontaneous emission occurs naturally without external influence, while stimulated emission occurs when an incoming photon causes an excited atom to emit a photon of the same energy, phase, and direction.

3. What is the significance of V-number in optical fibers?

Answer: The V-number determines the number of modes that can propagate in an optical fiber. For single-mode operation, V < 2.405; for multimode, V > 2.405.

4. Draw the refractive index profile of step index and graded index fibers.

Answer: Step index: The refractive index changes abruptly at the core-cladding boundary. Graded index: The refractive index decreases gradually from the center of the core to the cladding.

5. What is numerical aperture (NA) in optical fibers?

Answer: Numerical aperture is a measure of the light-gathering ability of an optical fiber, defined as NA = sqrt(ncore2 - ncladding2).

6. Why are Nd3+ ions doped into YAG crystal in Nd-YAG lasers?

Answer: Nd3+ ions provide the necessary energy levels for lasing action in the YAG crystal, enabling efficient laser operation.

7. What is the basic principle of laser action?

Answer: Laser action is based on the principle of stimulated emission, where an incoming photon induces the emission of another photon with identical properties.

8. What is the acceptance angle in an optical fiber?

Answer: The acceptance angle is the maximum angle at which light can enter the fiber and still be guided through total internal reflection.
