Unit 2: Lasers & Fiber Optics

10 Mark Questions

1. Based on Einstein's quantum theory of radiation, obtain Einstein's A and B coefficients and the ratio of spontaneous to stimulated emission.

Answer:

Einstein's A and B Coefficients:

  • A21: Probability per unit time of spontaneous emission from level 2 to 1.
  • B12: Probability per unit time of absorption from 1 to 2 (induced by radiation).
  • B21: Probability per unit time of stimulated emission from 2 to 1 (induced by radiation).

Derivation:

  1. Consider a two-level system in thermal equilibrium with radiation.
  2. Write the rate equations for upward and downward transitions.
  3. Use Planck's law to relate the coefficients.
  4. Obtain the ratio of spontaneous to stimulated emission:
    \( \frac{A_{21}}{B_{21} \rho(\nu)} = e^{h\nu/kT} - 1 \)

Significance:

  • Spontaneous emission dominates at low photon densities (ordinary light).
  • Stimulated emission dominates at high photon densities (laser action).

2. With a neat energy level diagram, explain the working of a CO2 laser along with its applications.

Answer:

Energy Level Diagram:

  • CO2 laser uses vibrational energy levels of CO2 molecules.
  • Population inversion is achieved between asymmetric stretch and bending modes.

Working:

  1. Electrical discharge excites N2 molecules, which transfer energy to CO2.
  2. Population inversion occurs between upper and lower laser levels.
  3. Laser action occurs at 10.6 μm and 9.6 μm.

Applications:

  • Material cutting and welding
  • Medical surgery
  • Military and industrial uses

3. Demonstrate the classification of optical fibers based on different modes of propagation.

Answer:

Classification:

  • Single-mode fibers: Support only one propagation mode (V < 2.405).
  • Multimode fibers: Support multiple propagation modes (V > 2.405).

Types:

  • Step-index fiber: Abrupt change in refractive index at core-cladding boundary.
  • Graded-index fiber: Gradual change in refractive index from core center to cladding.

Significance:

  • Single-mode: Long-distance, high-bandwidth communication.
  • Multimode: Short-distance, lower bandwidth, easier coupling.

4. Derive the expression for acceptance angle and numerical aperture of an optical fiber.

Answer:

Acceptance Angle (θa):

  • Maximum angle at which light can enter the fiber and be guided.
  • Given by: \( \sin \theta_a = \sqrt{n_{core}^2 - n_{cladding}^2} \)

Numerical Aperture (NA):

  • NA = \( \sqrt{n_{core}^2 - n_{cladding}^2} \)
  • Determines light-gathering ability and number of modes.
