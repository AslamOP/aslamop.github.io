Unit 2: Lasers & Fiber Optics

15 Mark Questions

1. Explain the process of population inversion and pumping in lasers. Discuss the different pumping methods used in lasers with examples.

Answer:

Population Inversion:

  • Population inversion is the condition where more atoms are in an excited state than in the ground state.
  • Essential for laser action to achieve net stimulated emission.

Pumping Methods:

  • Optical Pumping: Using light (e.g., flash lamps) to excite atoms (e.g., Ruby laser).
  • Electrical Pumping: Using electric discharge (e.g., He-Ne, CO₂ lasers).
  • Chemical Pumping: Using chemical reactions (e.g., chemical lasers).
  • Direct Conversion: Using electrical current in semiconductors (e.g., diode lasers).

Examples:

  • Nd:YAG laser (optical pumping)
  • CO₂ laser (electrical pumping)
  • Semiconductor laser (direct conversion)

2. With neat diagrams, explain the construction and working of Nd:YAG, CO₂, and semiconductor lasers. Mention their applications.

Answer:

Nd:YAG Laser:

  • Solid-state laser using Neodymium-doped Yttrium Aluminum Garnet as the active medium.
  • Optically pumped, four-level system.
  • Applications: Material processing, medical surgery, range finding.

CO₂ Laser:

  • Gas laser using CO₂ as the active medium.
  • Electrically pumped, vibrational energy transitions.
  • Applications: Cutting, welding, medical procedures.

Semiconductor Laser:

  • Uses p-n junction as the active region.
  • Direct electrical pumping.
  • Applications: Optical communication, barcode scanners, laser pointers.

3. Explain the structure, types, and applications of optical fibers. Discuss the advantages of optical fiber communication over conventional systems.

Answer:

Structure:

  • Core, cladding, and protective coating.
  • Total internal reflection guides light through the core.

Types:

  • Step-index and graded-index fibers.
  • Single-mode and multimode fibers.

Applications:

  • Telecommunications, internet, medical imaging, sensors.

Advantages:

  • High bandwidth, low loss, immunity to electromagnetic interference, secure data transmission.

4. Derive the expression for numerical aperture and explain its significance in optical fibers.

Answer:

Numerical Aperture (NA):

  • NA = sqrt(ncore2 - ncladding2)
  • Determines the light-gathering ability and number of modes in the fiber.
  • Higher NA: More light accepted, more modes (multimode fiber).
  • Lower NA: Single mode, less dispersion, better for long-distance communication.

5. Discuss the advantages and applications of lasers and optical fibers in modern technology. Compare optical fiber communication with conventional communication systems.

Answer:

Advantages of Lasers:

  • High intensity, coherence, monochromaticity, directionality.
  • Precision in cutting, welding, surgery, measurement.

Applications of Lasers:

  • Industry (cutting, welding, marking)
  • Medicine (surgery, dermatology, ophthalmology)
  • Communication (fiber optics, data transfer)
  • Research (spectroscopy, atomic physics)

Advantages of Optical Fibers:

  • High bandwidth, low loss, immunity to EMI, secure data transmission.
  • Lightweight, flexible, easy installation.

Applications of Optical Fibers:

  • Telecommunications, internet, medical imaging, sensors.

Comparison with Conventional Communication:

  • Optical fiber: Higher bandwidth, lower loss, more secure, less interference.
  • Conventional (copper): Lower bandwidth, higher loss, susceptible to EMI.
