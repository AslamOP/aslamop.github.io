Unit 3: Dielectric & Magnetic Materials

2 Mark Questions

1. What is a dielectric material? Give examples.

Answer: A dielectric material is an electrical insulator that can be polarized by an electric field. Examples: glass, mica, ceramic, plastic.

2. Define dielectric constant.

Answer: Dielectric constant (relative permittivity) is the ratio of the permittivity of a material to the permittivity of free space (εr = ε/ε0).

3. What is polarization in dielectrics?

Answer: Polarization is the alignment of electric dipoles within a dielectric material when placed in an electric field.

4. What is the difference between polar and non-polar dielectrics?

Answer: Polar dielectrics have permanent dipole moments (e.g., water), while non-polar dielectrics do not (e.g., nitrogen, oxygen).

5. Define magnetic susceptibility.

Answer: Magnetic susceptibility (χ) is a measure of how much a material will become magnetized in an applied magnetic field.

6. What is the difference between ferromagnetic and paramagnetic materials?

Answer: Ferromagnetic materials have strong, permanent magnetization (e.g., iron), while paramagnetic materials have weak, temporary magnetization (e.g., aluminum).

7. What is Curie temperature?

Answer: Curie temperature is the temperature above which a ferromagnetic material loses its permanent magnetism and becomes paramagnetic.

8. What is the dielectric loss?

Answer: Dielectric loss is the energy dissipated as heat in a dielectric material when it is subjected to a varying electric field.
