Unit 3: Dielectric & Magnetic Materials

15 Mark Questions

1. Explain the different types of polarization in dielectrics. Derive the expression for electronic polarization and discuss temperature dependence. (PYQ)

Answer:

Types of Polarization:

  • Electronic polarization
  • Ionic polarization
  • Orientation polarization
  • Space charge polarization

Derivation:

Electronic polarization: p = αeE, where αe = 4πε₀R³

Temperature Dependence:

Electronic polarization is temperature independent.

2. Define dielectric constant. Explain the different types of dielectric materials and their applications. (PYQ)

Answer:

Dielectric Constant:

εr = ε/ε0

Types:

  • Polar dielectrics
  • Non-polar dielectrics

Applications:

  • Capacitors
  • Insulators
  • Transducers

3. What is dielectric loss? Explain the factors affecting dielectric loss and its significance in materials. (PYQ)

Answer:

Dielectric Loss:

Energy dissipated as heat in a dielectric under AC field.

Factors:

  • Frequency
  • Temperature
  • Material type

Significance:

  • Limits use in high-frequency applications

4. Explain the classification of magnetic materials. Discuss the properties and applications of ferromagnetic, paramagnetic, and diamagnetic materials. (PYQ)

Answer:

Classification:

  • Diamagnetic
  • Paramagnetic
  • Ferromagnetic

Properties & Applications:

  • Ferromagnetic: Strong, permanent magnetization (e.g., iron, transformers)
  • Paramagnetic: Weak, temporary magnetization (e.g., aluminum, MRI)
  • Diamagnetic: Weak, repelled by magnetic field (e.g., bismuth, magnetic levitation)

5. What is Curie temperature? Explain the behavior of ferromagnetic materials above and below Curie temperature. (PYQ)

Answer:

Curie Temperature:

Temperature above which ferromagnetic material becomes paramagnetic.

Behavior:

  • Below: Strong magnetization
  • Above: Loses permanent magnetism

6. Explain polarization and magnetization mechanisms in materials. Compare their similarities and differences. (Syllabus)

Answer:

Polarization:

Alignment of electric dipoles in dielectrics.

Magnetization:

Alignment of magnetic dipoles in magnetic materials.

Comparison:

  • Both involve dipole alignment
  • Polarization: Electric field; Magnetization: Magnetic field
