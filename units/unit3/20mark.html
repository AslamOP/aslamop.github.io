Unit 3: Dielectric & Magnetic Materials

20 Mark Questions

1. Explain in detail the mechanisms of polarization in dielectrics. Discuss the significance of dielectric constant and dielectric loss, and their applications in engineering. (PYQ/Syllabus)

Answer: Mechanisms: Electronic, ionic, orientation, space charge. Dielectric constant: εr = ε/ε0. Dielectric loss: energy dissipated as heat. Applications: capacitors, insulators, transducers.

2. Classify magnetic materials and explain the properties, behavior, and applications of ferromagnetic, paramagnetic, and diamagnetic materials. (PYQ/Syllabus)

Answer: Ferromagnetic: strong, permanent (iron, transformers); Paramagnetic: weak, temporary (aluminum, MRI); Diamagnetic: weak, repelled (bismuth, levitation).

3. What is Curie temperature? Explain the behavior of ferromagnetic materials above and below Curie temperature. Discuss its importance in magnetic applications. (PYQ)

Answer: Curie temperature: above this, ferromagnetic becomes paramagnetic. Below: strong magnetization; above: loses magnetism. Importance: limits use in devices.

4. Compare and contrast polarization in dielectrics and magnetization in magnetic materials. (Syllabus)

Answer: Both involve dipole alignment. Polarization: electric dipoles, electric field; Magnetization: magnetic dipoles, magnetic field.

5. Discuss the engineering applications of dielectric and magnetic materials in modern technology. (Syllabus)

Answer: Dielectrics: capacitors, insulators, sensors. Magnetics: transformers, data storage, MRI, motors.
